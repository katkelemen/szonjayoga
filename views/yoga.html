Why should you practice yoga?

The word ‘yoga’ means ‘to unite’ which refers to the union with the divine and also unite of the mind, body and spirit. The history of yoga stretches back about 5000 years in India where the exercises and poses were more about to prepare the body for the long, seated mediations. Yoga is a discipline and also a philosophy.

Yoga is not just about working on placing one leg behind the head whilst being upside down poses. Regular practice is able to give much more than flexibility and a stronger body. The good news is that people can do yoga regardless their ages, sizes, shapes and abilities and adapt it to suit their individual needs.

So what can yoga offer?

Regular yoga practice tones, strengthens and stretches the muscles. It also increases the proprioception and improves balance. During the day many people have poor posture which can cause neck, back pain and other muscular and join problems. I am sure we all know someone who sits hours at a table in front of a computer… With a daily yoga practice you can relieve your body pain and perfect your posture.

Yoga poses, pranayama (breathing techniques) and meditation are all effective techniques to release the stress, fear and anxiety of modern life. Due to this fact, yoga improves sleep quality and helps people with insomnia.

Another benefit of yoga is that it boosts your immunity as well. Everything is connected, as you start to focus on your breath and your movements, you allow your mind to be in the present moment, you calm your mind, you release stress and your immunity becomes better. As a result of being in tune with your body, helps to increase your self-confidence. Simply and shortly yoga makes you happier.

Vinyasa Flow

Vinyasa Flow is a dynamic style yoga where breath and movements are synchronised. The poses are smoothly and gracefully linked into each other and they become like a dance. Because of its pace it requires the mind to stay in the present moment.

Vinyasa Flow Yoga as other styles of yoga has physical and mental benefits. Just a few examples of many: relaxes the nervous system, improves brain function, strengthens muscles and increases flexibility.

I find it really important to give options and variations during my classes as well as lots of cues and alignments. In this way everyone can choose the option which feels the best for him/her in that moment.

I love motivating others to succeed and intend to create a safe environment for my students in order to support inward focus and observation of the body, mind and spirit.

Special Yoga for Special Children

Yoga can be really beneficial for children whether they have special needs or not. During yoga sessions I teach different yoga poses, pranayama (breathing techniques) and relaxation. Every element of the session is tailor-made to individual needs and abilities. I believe that it is important to create a safe, calm and quiet environment for children that will helps them to feel relaxed and secure. This state is ideal for positive changes.
